GAME RULES

• SURVIVAL MECHANICS
  - Monitor your Health, Stamina, Hunger, and System Exposure at all times
  - Health depletes when taking damage from enemies or environmental hazards
  - Stamina drains while running and regenerates when walking or standing still
  - Hunger decreases over time and must be managed through consumable items
  - System Exposure acts as a timer: when it reaches zero, you take continuous damage
  - If Health reaches zero, you die and the game ends

• COMBAT SYSTEM
  - Engage enemies using the Attack action (left mouse button)
  - All characters (player and enemies) have Health stats that decrease when damaged
  - When Health reaches minimum, characters die and are removed from the game
  - Use movement and positioning to avoid enemy attacks
  - Sneaking (Left Control) allows quieter movement but doesn't require stamina

• QUEST SYSTEM
  - Interact with NPCs to receive quests
  - Quests have objectives that must be completed (usually collecting specific items)
  - Complete quest objectives to finish quests and receive rewards
  - Rewards may include items or crafting recipes
  - You can accept or reject quests when offered
  - Track active quests in the Quest Overview panel (Q key)

• CRAFTING SYSTEM
  - Access crafting through the inventory interface
  - Recipes require specific ingredients in exact quantities
  - You must have all required ingredients in your inventory to craft
  - Crafted items are automatically added to your inventory
  - Some recipes are unlocked by completing quests
  - Inventory must have space for crafted items

• INVENTORY MANAGEMENT
  - Collect items from the environment and defeated enemies
  - Items can stack if they are the same type
  - Inventory has limited space - manage it carefully
  - Use consumable items to restore health, stamina, or hunger
  - Some items provide stat effects when used

• ENVIRONMENTAL INTERACTION
  - Interact with objects using the Interact action (hold key)
  - Tiles degrade when you walk or run on them - running causes more degradation
  - Broken tiles can be dangerous - watch your step
  - Use your torch (F key) to illuminate dark areas
  - Procedurally generated environments mean each playthrough is unique

• PROGRESSION
  - Complete quests to unlock new crafting recipes
  - Collect items to craft better equipment and consumables
  - Manage your resources carefully to survive longer
  - Explore procedurally generated dungeons to find valuable resources
  - Attributes can modify your stats (Vitality increases max health, Agility affects speed)

• DEATH AND FAILURE
  - Death occurs when Health reaches zero
  - System Exposure reaching zero causes continuous damage until death
  - Manage all survival stats to avoid death
  - Plan your exploration and resource management carefully


GAME SPECS

• MOVEMENT SPEEDS
  - Walking Speed: 7.5 units/second (default movement)
  - Running Speed: 11.5 units/second (requires stamina, hold Left Shift)
  - Sneaking Speed: 3.75 units/second (stealth movement, hold Left Control)
  - Jump Speed: 8.0 units/second (vertical velocity)
  - Look Speed: 2.0 (mouse sensitivity multiplier)
  - Look Limit: ±45 degrees (vertical camera rotation limit)

• STATISTICS SYSTEM
  - Health: Core survival stat, decreases from damage, reaches zero = death
  - Stamina: Drains at 10/second while running, regenerates at 5/second when not running
  - Speed: Base movement speed modifier
  - Hunger: Player-specific stat that decreases over time
  - System Exposure: Player-specific stat that acts as environmental timer (default 180 seconds to drain)

• COMBAT SPECIFICATIONS
  - Attack Action: Left mouse button
  - All entities implement IDamageable interface
  - Damage reduces Health stat directly
  - Death occurs when Health.current <= Health.min

• TILE DEGRADATION
  - Walking on tiles causes degradation (walkDegradationAmount)
  - Running on tiles causes increased degradation (runDegradationAmount)
  - Tiles can break when degradation reaches maximum
  - Broken tiles are dangerous and cannot be further degraded
  - Tile interaction has 0.1 second cooldown to prevent rapid degradation

• TORCH SYSTEM
  - Toggle Key: F (default)
  - Intensity: 3.0 (light brightness)
  - Range: 18 units
  - Spot Angle: 60 degrees (light cone width)
  - Color: Warm white (RGB: 1.0, 0.95, 0.85)
  - Shadows: Soft shadows enabled

• CRAFTING SPECIFICATIONS
  - Recipes require exact ingredient quantities
  - Crafting checks inventory for all required ingredients
  - Crafted items added in specified quantities (default: 1)
  - Inventory space required for crafted items
  - Failed crafts do not consume ingredients

• UI SYSTEM
  - Inventory: I key (toggle)
  - Quest Overview: Q key (toggle)
  - Journal: J key (toggle)
  - Close UI: X key (when in UI mode)
  - Cursor locked during gameplay, unlocked in UI mode
  - Player movement disabled when UI is open

• ATTRIBUTE TYPES
  - Vitality: Increases maximum Health (+10 per attribute value)
  - Agility: Modifies Speed stat
  - Other attribute types may exist for stat modification

• GRAVITY AND PHYSICS
  - Gravity: 20.0 units/second² (downward acceleration)
  - Character Controller handles collision and movement
  - Camera offset: 0.4 units above player position
  - Camera near clip: 0.1 units, far clip: 2000 units, FOV: 75 degrees


PLAYER INSTRUCTIONS - HOW TO PLAY

• BASIC CONTROLS
  - WASD: Move forward, left, backward, right
  - Mouse: Look around (camera rotation)
  - Space: Jump
  - Left Shift: Hold to run (drains stamina)
  - Left Control: Hold to sneak (slower, quieter movement)
  - Left Mouse Button: Attack
  - Hold E (or configured Interact key): Interact with objects
  - F: Toggle torch on/off

• UI NAVIGATION
  - Press I to open/close Inventory
  - Press Q to open/close Quest Overview
  - Press J to open/close Journal
  - Press X to close any open UI panel
  - Mouse cursor appears when UI is open
  - Click buttons and items in UI panels to interact

• SURVIVAL TIPS
  - Keep an eye on your Health bar - it's your lifeline
  - Manage Stamina: don't run constantly, let it regenerate
  - Watch System Exposure: it's a timer for environmental danger
  - Use consumable items from inventory to restore stats
  - Hunger decreases over time - find and use food items

• EXPLORATION STRATEGY
  - Use your torch (F) in dark areas to see better
  - Walk on tiles to preserve them longer (running degrades them faster)
  - Watch for broken tiles - they can be dangerous
  - Explore procedurally generated areas to find resources
  - Interact with NPCs to receive quests

• QUEST COMPLETION
  - Talk to NPCs marked as quest givers
  - Read quest descriptions carefully to understand objectives
  - Collect required items specified in quest objectives
  - Check Quest Overview (Q) to track progress
  - Complete objectives to finish quests and earn rewards
  - Rewards may include items or new crafting recipes

• CRAFTING GUIDE
  - Open Inventory (I) to access crafting
  - View available recipes and their required ingredients
  - Ensure you have all required ingredients before crafting
  - Click craft button to create items
  - Crafted items automatically appear in inventory
  - Some recipes unlock through quest completion

• COMBAT TACTICS
  - Use Attack (left mouse) to engage enemies
  - Move while fighting to avoid taking damage
  - Monitor your Health during combat
  - Use consumables to heal if Health gets low
  - Sneak (Left Control) to avoid detection if possible

• INVENTORY MANAGEMENT
  - Collect items from environment and defeated enemies
  - Organize items - same types stack automatically
  - Use consumable items by clicking "Use" button in inventory
  - Keep inventory space available for important items
  - Drop or use items you don't need to free space

• ADVANCED TECHNIQUES
  - Balance running and walking to manage stamina efficiently
  - Plan routes to minimize tile degradation
  - Complete quests early to unlock better crafting options
  - Save consumables for critical moments
  - Use torch strategically to conserve resources (if applicable)
  - Monitor all stats simultaneously for optimal survival
